SDT-HBM: Smart Digital Twin Platform for High-Rise Building Management in Dubai

AI-Driven Smart Digital Twin Platform for High-Rise Buildings in Dubai

Project Overview

The SDT-HBM project (Smart Digital Twin for High-Rise Building Management) aims to build an AI-driven platform that fuses IoT data, Building Information Models (BIM), and advanced analytics to create a living, virtual replica of high-rise buildings in Dubai. This digital twin continuously mirrors the building’s state in terms of energy use, indoor environmental quality, equipment health, and occupant comfort, enabling operators to move from reactive control to proactive, data-driven decision-making.

At the core of the platform are machine learning and deep learning models for short-term energy forecasting, anomaly detection, and joint comfort–energy prediction. These models provide calibrated uncertainty estimates, identify abnormal operation and potential faults, and quantify the trade-offs between energy savings and occupant comfort. The offline app built for this project showcases this AI core by analyzing historical or simulated building data and generating actionable insights that would later be fed into the live digital twin.

In its full deployment, SDT-HBM will integrate real-time data from IoT sensors and BMS systems, visualize performance in 3D BIM environments, and support predictive maintenance and control strategies aligned with Dubai’s Net Zero 2050 goals. The long-term vision is to create a scalable blueprint for AI-enabled digital twins that can be replicated across high-rise clusters and smart districts in Dubai and beyond.

Project Objectives

  • Real-time monitoring of high-rise building energy, comfort, and environmental quality.
  • AI-driven predictive maintenance and anomaly detection.
  • Joint energy–comfort optimization aligned with Net Zero 2050.
  • Scalable digital twin integration using BIM and IoT.
  • Deployment-ready platform for Dubai’s high-rise infrastructure.

Expected Impact

  • Reduce building operational energy consumption by up to 25%.
  • Increase comfort compliance in occupied zones.
  • Enable proactive fault detection before equipment failure.
  • Support data-driven smart city planning.
  • Establish Dubai as a global leader in urban digital twins.
