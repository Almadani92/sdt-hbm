SDT-HBM: Smart Digital Twin Platform for High-Rise Building Management in Dubai

An AI-driven digital twin platform that fuses IoT data, BIM models, and advanced analytics to monitor, predict, and optimize the performance of high-rise buildings across Dubai.

The SDT-HBM project (Smart Digital Twin for High-Rise Building Management) aims to build an AI-driven platform that fuses IoT data, Building Information Models (BIM), and advanced analytics to create a living, virtual replica of high-rise buildings in Dubai. This digital twin mirrors energy use, indoor environmental quality, equipment health, and occupant comfort, enabling a shift from reactive control to proactive, data-driven decision-making.

At the core of the platform are machine learning and deep learning models for short-term energy forecasting, anomaly detection, and joint comfort–energy prediction. These models provide calibrated uncertainty estimates, identify abnormal operation and potential faults, and quantify the trade-offs between energy savings and occupant comfort.

In its full deployment, SDT-HBM will connect to real-time IoT and BMS data, visualize performance in 3D BIM environments, and support predictive maintenance and control strategies aligned with Dubai’s Net Zero 2050 goals — forming a scalable blueprint for AI-enabled digital twins across districts and cities.

Funding & Strategic Focus
Sponsored by Dubai Future Foundation under the Dubai RDI Program, focusing on Smart Built Infrastructure, Cognitive Cities, and Net Zero 2050.
Core Objectives
  • Real-time monitoring of high-rise building energy, comfort, and environmental quality.
  • AI-driven predictive maintenance and anomaly detection.
  • Joint energy–comfort optimization aligned with Net Zero 2050.
  • Scalable digital twin integration using BIM and IoT.
  • Deployment-ready platform for Dubai’s high-rise infrastructure.
Expected Impact
  • Reduce building operational energy consumption by up to 25%.
  • Increase comfort compliance in occupied zones.
  • Enable proactive fault detection before equipment failure.
  • Support data-driven smart city planning and governance.
  • Position Dubai as a global leader in urban digital twin technologies.
